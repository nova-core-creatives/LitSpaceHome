An SSL certificate is a digital certificate that validates a website's identity and enables an encrypted connection. Secure Sockets Layer (SSL) is a security protocol that establishes an encrypted link between a web server and a web browser. To secure online transactions and protect consumer information, companies and organizations must add SSL certificates to their websites. Learn More
The server is secured by a number of SSL security certificates, as well as filtered firewall rules for unused ports, and multi-factor authentication on the root. Only one method into the backend exists, and the keys are kept offline in case of a compromised machine. To safeguard against malware and targeted assaults, we use monitoring and anti-virus technologies.

Tools

A number of tools have been added to your dashboard under "Dev Tools". We'll briefly discuss a few of the most important features at your disposal and why they are important.

  1. Google Pagespeed Insights


    Along with the SEO toolkit on the side panel, the Pagespeed insights is a tool run by Google to give you a heads up on the speed of your website. It gives you both mobile and desktop scores and ways to improve upon your current score. If you are working with us, this score is taken into account while we build your site so you will not have to configure this manually.

  2. Speed Kit


    This tool can quickly wrap your site into more deliverable packages. It is best used by pre-existing sites that have transferred hosting to our servers and would like a speed boost. This tool is available upon request.

  3. ImunifyAV


    This preconfigured tool constantly scans all of your sites for malware and malicious code; keeping you safe from attack and loss of security.

  4. File Manager


    Think of this as cloud storage for your website. This is where all of the files that make up your site are held including images and code. You are able to access any of this content and move pages from one place.

DNS Records

When you are ready to set up your website on your domain, you'll have to tell your registrar that you have changed locations. This is fairly simple, and recommend changing what's called "NameServers" to point at your new host.
Changing your nameservers benefits you in a few ways. For one, it leaves your current domain registered with your orginial provider. This way, you can switch hosting services at anytime, even if you have forgotten you password to your hosting portal. It also provides you the flexibility to add records from your control panel and will make your site load faster!

Administrator Access

Any web designer you hire will need access to the global permissions granted to an administrator.

Your first instinct might be to invite your designer as a "Website Editor", and that is a fair assumption, but you have to think of the editor permissions like a newspaper editor. They don't make the website, just change and adjust content.

Without administrator permissions, your designer will not be able to change the colors, fonts, menus, or structure of your site. Essesntially rendering their services moot.
For more information regarding permssions, checkout SquareSpace's Documentation: Learn More
